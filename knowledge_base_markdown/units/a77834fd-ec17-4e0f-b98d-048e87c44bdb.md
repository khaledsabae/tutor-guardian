> DSM is used to assess and diagnose  mental disorders. For more information on DMDD,  visit www.nimh.nih.gov/dmdd.  What is the difference between  typical and severe irritability?  All children can become irritable sometimes.  It’s a normal reaction to frustration. Children  experiencing severe irritability (as observed in  DMDD) have difficulty tolerating frustration and  have outbursts that are out of proportion to the  situation at hand.
