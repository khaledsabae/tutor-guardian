# Confidence and Identity: You are His Child and Allah Sees You

### 1. Lesson Overview and Core Objectives
Assalamu Alaikum, dear parents! At the ages of 7 to 9, our children are blossoming into their own unique personalities. This is a beautiful window of time to nurture their hearts with a sense of belonging and confidence. Our goal is to help them understand that their identity is rooted in a special relationship with their Creator.

The core objectives of this lesson are:
*   **Embracing Islamic Identity:** Helping your child realize that they are a precious creation of Allah, which forms the foundation of their self-worth.
*   **Navigating Social Worlds:** Giving your child the tools to interact with friends and family using the beautiful manners (Adab) taught in our faith.
*   **Understanding Big Feelings:** Learning to identify when emotional struggles, like tantrums, are health-related and may need expert care.
*   **Building Character:** Encouraging a sense of pride in doing good deeds, from telling the truth to helping a neighbor.

### 2. Foundations of Islamic Identity
In the journey of raising our children, we rely on the **Basics of Islamic Education** to provide them with a clear sense of who they are. When a child understands the concept of *"Allah sees you,"* it isn't meant to be scary; rather, it is a comforting reminder that they are never alone and are always cared for by their Creator.

*   **The Holy Quran:** This is our primary guidebook. We share its stories to help children see the world through the lens of faith and wisdom.
*   **Islamic Creed (Aqidah):** For a 7-9-year-old, this simply means knowing in our hearts who Allah is, understanding His love for us, and feeling a deep sense of belonging to the Muslim family.
*   **Islamic Ethics:** This is how we turn our faith into action. It is the practice of being kind, honest, and respectful because we want to please Allah.

### 3. The Role of the Parent: A Balanced Approach
As parents, you are the most important mirror for your child. They see their own value reflected in your eyes. To help them grow, the source suggests a gentle balance between cheering them on and keeping a watchful, loving eye.

**The Parental Balance**

| Motivation (Stimulation) | Supervision (Monitoring) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Acting as a Source of Value:** Being a positive role model and showing your child they are worthy of love and respect. | **The "Allah Sees You" Concept:** Teaching children to be mindful of their actions, knowing that Allah is always watching over them with care. |
| **Encouraging Growth:** Using praise and excitement to motivate the child to keep trying their best in their religious and social life. | **Consistent Guidance:** Gently observing the child’s behavior to ensure they are staying on a healthy path of development. |

### 4. Social Education and Behavioral Guidance
Social education is about teaching our children how to live beautifully among others. Many of these principles are found in the classic guide *Tarbiyat al-Awlad* (Raising Children in Islam) by Abdullah Nasheed Ulwan. This book reminds us that teaching religious knowledge must go hand-in-hand with teaching social behavior and ethics.

However, we must also be mindful of our children’s health. Sometimes, behavior is more than just a "phase."

> **A Note on Temper Tantrums**
> It is important to understand that temper tantrums are health issues that can affect children and even healthy adults. If you notice that tantrums are becoming severe or very frequent, the source recommends consulting with a mental health specialist to provide your child (or family) with the right support and treatment.

### 5. Practical Application: The "Achievement Box" Activity
Let’s make goodness visible! This activity helps your child see the "evidence" of their wonderful character, building a healthy sense of pride and self-image.

1.  **Create the Box:** Find a small box and decorate it together with glitter, stickers, or drawings. Label it "[Child's Name]'s Achievement Box."
2.  **Daily Recording:** Every evening, ask your child to write down one thing they did that day that made them feel proud. It could be finishing a prayer, being honest when it was hard, or helping someone.
3.  **Monthly Review:** At the end of every month, sit down together in a "celebration circle" and read the notes out loud. This reinforces their identity as a person who does good and strives to follow Islamic ethics.

### 6. Reflections for Growth
As you go through this month with your child, take a moment of quiet time to think about these two questions:

*   *What was written in the achievement box?*
*   *Have you noticed a change in how your child talks about themselves?*

By focusing on these small shifts, you are helping your child build a heart that is confident in its faith and secure in its identity. Remember: you are raising a child who is loved by Allah, and that is the greatest source of confidence they will ever have.
